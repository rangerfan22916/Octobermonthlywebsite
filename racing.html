Enzo Ferrari's racing career began in the early 1920s when he joined Alfa Romeo as a test driver and race car driver. His talent on the track quickly earned him recognition, and he became a successful competitor in various racing events. Enzo achieved his first major victory in 1924 at the Targa Florio, a prestigious Italian race. Throughout the 1920s and 1930s, he continued to race for Alfa Romeo, winning numerous titles and establishing himself as one of the top drivers of his time. However, after a serious accident in 1932 that nearly cost him his life, he decided to shift his focus from driving to managing and building race cars. This transition laid the groundwork for his future as the founder of Ferrari, where he would channel his racing passion into developing high-performance vehicles and leading the Ferrari team to victory in many prestigious races, including Formula One.
